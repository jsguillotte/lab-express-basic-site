The Life of Dr. Seuss

Dr. Seuss, whose real name was Theodor Seuss Geisel, was an American author, cartoonist, and animator. He was born on March 2, 1904, in Springfield, Massachusetts. Dr. Seuss is best known for his imaginative and whimsical stories, captivating both children and adults alike.

Throughout his career, Dr. Seuss wrote and illustrated numerous classic books, including "The Cat in the Hat," "Green Eggs and Ham," "Horton Hears a Who!," and many more. His works continue to inspire generations and promote values of creativity, acceptance, and kindness.
